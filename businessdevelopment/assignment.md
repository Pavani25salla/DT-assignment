## About This Role

Every BD person at DeepThought does one thing: **get the right MD on a call and make them care about Ownership Hiring.**

Your day looks like this:
- You receive company research from our business analysts — company profile, MD details, recent hiring activity, signals that suggest they need an ownership hire
- You read the dossier, plan your approach, and call the MD
- You have 60-90 seconds before they decide whether to keep listening or hang up
- If they push back, you handle the objection and try to book a deeper conversation
- If they're interested, you send a crisp follow-up and hand off to the senior team

You don't source companies. You don't write research. You don't close deals. You call, you pitch, you handle "no," and you open doors.

This assignment tests exactly that.

### Part A: The Pitch Voice Note (50%)

You are given a company dossier in `context.md` — **Company A (Naveen Chemicals)**. Read it carefully. Then record a voice note.

The scenario: you are calling Rajesh Mehta, the MD of Naveen Chemicals. He picked up the phone. Go.

**Duration:** 60-90 seconds. Not a speech. A phone call opening.

**Language:** Hindi, English, or your natural mix — whatever you'd actually say on a real call. If you normally speak Hinglish on the phone, speak Hinglish. If you're more comfortable in English, speak English. Don't perform. Be yourself.

#### Guidelines — read these carefully

1. **Don't read a script.** We can tell instantly. Bullet points for yourself are fine — full sentences read aloud are not. The delivery has to be conversational, the way you'd talk to a person, not the way you'd present to a class.

2. **Don't start with "Hi sir, my name is X, I'm calling from DeepThought, we are a company that..."** Every telecaller in India starts this way. The MD's brain shuts off at "I'm calling from." Find a better opening.

3. **Lead with a problem, not your product.** Name something the MD recognizes from his own experience. If he doesn't recognize the problem in the first 15 seconds, you've lost him.

4. **Personalize to Naveen Chemicals.** The dossier has specific details — Rajesh's background, why previous BD hires failed, what they're looking for now. Use them. A pitch that could work for any company is a pitch that works for no one.

5. **No corporate jargon.** No "leverage," no "synergies," no "value proposition," no "holistic solution," no "end-to-end talent management." If an MD in Ahmedabad would roll his eyes hearing it, don't say it. Talk like a person.

6. **Have a clear ask.** Don't just describe Hire90 and trail off. What do you want from this call? A meeting? 10 more minutes? Be specific.

#### What we're listening for

- **Would this MD give you 60 more seconds?** That's the only question. Everything else flows from it.
- Did you lead with a problem he recognizes, or a product description?
- Did you use the dossier, or deliver a generic pitch?
- Do you sound like someone who knows what they're talking about, or someone who memorized a brief this morning?
- Energy — not telecaller enthusiasm, but conviction. The difference between "I'm excited to tell you about our offering!" and "you've probably had this exact problem."

#### What kills your application

- Reading verbatim from a written script
- Sounding like a translated LLM output — formal, impersonal, American idiom, no contractions, sentences that no Indian person would actually say out loud
- Listing features instead of naming a pain
- No ask at the end — just trailing off after describing the product
- Starting with your company's credentials before the MD has any reason to care

### Part B: Objection Handling Voice Notes (30%)

After your pitch, the MD pushes back. Record a **separate voice note for each** of the 4 objections in `context.md` (Section 3). Respond as if the MD just said this to you on the phone.

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds per objection. Four separate voice notes.

Same rules as Part A: natural language, no script reading, conversational tone.

Read the context below each objection before recording — it explains why the MD says it and what they actually mean. Your response should address what they mean, not just what they said.

#### What we're listening for

- Do you acknowledge the objection or bulldoze past it?
- Does your response address what the MD actually means, not just the surface words?
- Do you recover the conversation toward a next step, or just defend your product?
- Does your energy hold under pushback, or do you sound rattled?

**Remember: you don't need to "win."** The goal is to get the MD to say "okay, tell me more" or "fine, let's set up a proper call." You're opening a door, not closing a deal.

### Part C: Pre-Call Prep Note (20%)

You're given a second company dossier in `context.md` — **Company B (Prism Precision Engineering)**. You're calling this MD in 10 minutes.

Write your call plan in **5-7 lines:**
- What's your opening line? (The first thing you'll say after the MD picks up)
- What pain point are you leading with?
- What's the one thing about Hire90 that connects to their specific situation?
- What's your ask?

This is not a script. It's the notes you'd scribble on a Post-it before dialing. Keep it rough and real.
