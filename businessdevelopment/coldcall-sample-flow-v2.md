# Hire90 — Cold Call Sample Flow (v2)
### Program: hire90 | April 29, 2026
### Source: coldcall-structure-v2.md
### Type: Hot-path dialogue for reviewing emotional flow and language.

---

**Company:** NovaBio Lifesciences, ₹180Cr specialty biotech. Just expanded to Southeast Asia. Decision-maker: Ramesh Iyer, PhD, IIT Bombay. Found a JD on LinkedIn: "Head of International Business Development — build the export pipeline from scratch, report to MD."

---

## Stage 1 — Greeting

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, this is Priya from DeepThought. Is this a good time?

**Ramesh:** Haan bolo, what is this about?

---

## Stage 2 — Introduction + Permission

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, I saw NovaBio opened in Southeast Asia recently. Congratulations on that. Sir, the reason I'm calling is we work with MDs and decision-makers who are trying to hire for a role that's tied to a growth number. Not a regular hire. The kind where the decision-maker is personally involved because the role is too important to leave to HR.

We've been talking to a lot of growing companies and there's a pattern we keep seeing. Can I take 2 minutes to share it?

**Ramesh:** Okay, tell me.

---

## Stage 3 — Positioning Statement

**Priya:** Sir, we keep hearing two situations from MDs and decision-makers.

First one is where the decision-maker knows the number that needs to move. Pipeline, conversion, margins, something specific. They can see the gap. But the role itself has never existed in the company. There is no JD for it. HR can post something, but nobody has mapped what this person actually does every day. So they end up hiring on impression. Three months later the person is busy but the number hasn't moved.

Second one is where they've already tried. Hired someone once, twice. Good start, slow second month, person is gone by month three. The decision-maker expected ownership. The person expected someone to tell them what to do. Nobody had sat down and defined what this role actually demands.

And sir, in both cases, the people are not the problem. Good people exist. The issue is that the hiring process was built for roles like accountant or plant manager, where the job is defined. It doesn't work for roles where the person has to own a number and figure things out themselves.

*(Pause.)*

**Ramesh:** That's actually the first situation for us. We've never had someone doing international BD. I've been writing the JD myself and honestly I don't even know what this person should be doing on a Monday morning.

---

## Stage 4 — Ownership Hiring: What Changes

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, that's exactly what we keep hearing. Do one thing, let me tell you what other companies in your situation have done.

Instead of posting the JD and hoping for the best, they first sit down and define the role properly against the KPI. What does this person do every day. What does success look like at day 30, day 60, day 90. What kind of person actually delivers in this seat. Then they test the candidate on the real job before hiring. They give them a real scenario from the business and watch them work it. Not an interview where someone talks well. They actually do the work.

One services company we worked with had a similar situation. BDR role. Never existed in the org. Nobody knew what the job looked like. We mapped the pipeline, defined the role properly, ran a simulation with real scenarios. The hire was productive from month one. Six months later they needed a second BDR and the whole process took half the time because the blueprint was already there.

We've also placed young people in these roles, sir. 24 years old, AI-native, tech-savvy. One of them picked up the entire pipeline in two weeks. Sometimes the right person for an ownership role is not the 15-year veteran. It's someone younger who actually takes ownership and doesn't carry baggage from a previous company.

**Ramesh:** Interesting. The simulation part, how does that work?

**Priya:** Good question, sir. That's something Tarun goes into in detail. But first let me understand if this is relevant for NovaBio's situation.

---

## Stage 5 — Qualification

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, can I ask a couple of quick questions?

**Ramesh:** Sure.

**Priya:** The Southeast Asia expansion. Is there a specific number attached to it? A pipeline target or revenue target?

**Ramesh:** We need ₹15Cr export pipeline in the next 12 months. Right now it's zero. Products are ready, registrations are done. But nobody is owning the business development part.

**Priya:** And sir, have you tried hiring for this kind of role before, or is this the first time?

**Ramesh:** First time for international BD. We hired a National Sales Manager two years ago. That didn't work out. Good person, but he just kept waiting for me to tell him what to do.

**Priya:** And the ₹15Cr pipeline, is this something that needs to happen this quarter itself, or is it a next-year conversation?

**Ramesh:** We need someone in seat by June. The registrations are done. Every month we don't have someone owning this, we're losing money.

---

## Stage 6 — Commitment

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, let me make sure I understood properly. NovaBio has ₹15Cr export pipeline to build. Zero right now. Registrations are done, products are ready, but nobody is owning the BD side. You need someone by June. And the last time you hired for something similar, the National Sales Manager, he waited for instructions instead of taking ownership.

Sir, what you're describing is an ownership role. This is not something you can fill by posting on Naukri and running three rounds of interviews. The role needs to be designed from the KPI backwards.

This is exactly what our founder Tarun does. He has done this across biotech, pharma, AdTech, services companies. He sits with the decision-maker, maps the role against the KPI, designs the simulation, and makes sure you get someone who actually owns the number.

Sir, would it make sense for you to have a 30-minute call with Tarun? No commitment. He will walk you through what applies to NovaBio and what the right approach looks like.

**Ramesh:** Yeah, set it up. This week if possible.

**Priya:** Done, sir. Thursday or Friday, which works?

**Ramesh:** Thursday morning.

---

## Stage 7 — Thank You

**Priya:** Ramesh ji, thank you for your time. I will send you a WhatsApp with the Thursday confirmation, a link to our website that has both case studies with numbers, and a short note on what we discussed. And sir, if you know any other MD or decision-maker who is dealing with a similar kind of hire, do share it with them. It clicks when someone is in that situation.

**Ramesh:** Thanks, Priya. Send it over.
