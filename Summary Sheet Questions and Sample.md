# Summary Sheet Questions and Sample **Answers**

#### **1. What does this HRMS exist to deliver, and to whom?**

#### **Answer**: This HRMS exists to ensure accurate and timely payroll processing for construction workers. Construction workers depend on it most because their salary and financial stability rely on correct payroll calculations.

#### 

#### **2. What is the single most dangerous bug pattern you found across your testing?**

#### **Answer**: The most dangerous bug pattern is incorrect attendance data flowing into payroll calculations. A single attendance error can affect salary payments for multiple workers and lead to payroll disputes.

#### 

#### **3. Which test in your suite are you most proud of, and why?**

#### **Answer:** I am most proud of the attendance-to-payroll validation test because it ensures attendance records are correctly reflected in salary calculations. This prevents workers from being underpaid or overpaid.

#### 

#### **4. What did you choose NOT to automate, and why was that the right call?**

#### **Answer:** I choose not to automate rarely used administrative settings because they change infrequently and provide less business value compared to payroll-related workflows. Focusing on critical payroll flows it provides better risk coverage.



#### **5. What's one thing in your submission that you're not fully confident about, and why did you include it anyway?**

#### **Answer:** I am not fully confident about some construction payroll domain rules because I am new to the industry. I included my assumptions after researching the workflow and clearly documented them.



#### **6. What changed between your first approach and your final submission?**

#### **Answer:** Initially, I focused on generic HRMS testing. After further analysis, I revised my work to focus on attendance, payroll accuracy, overtime calculations, and construction-worker impact.



#### **7. What do you not know about quality engineering or construction payroll that you would need to learn?**

#### **Answer:** I need to learn more about construction payroll regulations, statutory deductions, and large-scale test automation practices. These areas are important for handling real production systems.



#### **8. If you had one more day, what would you test that you didn't get to?**

#### **Answer:** I would test payroll processing under high employee volumes and concurrent user activity. This is important because payroll operators need the system to remain reliable during salary-processing periods.
